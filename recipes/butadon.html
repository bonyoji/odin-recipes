Tokachi Butadon (Pork Bowl)

Butadon is a comforting dish that originates from Tokachi, a region in the northern Japanese island of Hokkaido. Made with succulent pork belly fried in a caramel soy sauce glaze and served on top of Japanese steamed white rice, not only is it delicious, but itâ€™s also quick and easy to make!

Ingredients

  • 3/4 LB PORK SLICES
  • 1 TBS SOY SAUCE
  • 1 TBS MIRIN
  • 1 TBS SAKE
  • 2 TBS SUGAR
  • 2 TBS WATER
  • 1 TBS OIL
  • GREEN ONION GARNISH
  • SERVING RICE

Instructions

  1. Gather the ingredients
  2. Prepare the sauce. Add soy sauce, mirin, and sake together. Set aside
  3. Add sugar and water to a pan and heat up. Once caramelized, turn off heat
  4. Add the prepared sauce and combine. Set aside for later
  5. Heat oil in a pan. Add pork and cook until ready
  6. Once pork is cooked, add the prepared sauce and simmer until thickened
  7. Put pork on rice and garnish with green onions
  8. Enjoy!
